\begin{abstract}
We present an empirical evaluation of prompt injection and jailbreak
robustness across four models from the Llama family spanning three
orders of magnitude in parameter count: \tinyllama{} (1.1B),
\llamaA{} (8B), \llamaB{} (70B), and \llamaC{} (70B).
Using a structured test suite of 201 adversarial prompts across
10 attack categories and 4 severity levels, we measure pass rates
and identify failure patterns within and across generations.

Our key finding is that safety robustness does not scale
monotonically: \llamaC{} achieves a pass rate of 51.7\%, which is
19 percentage points \emph{lower} than \llamaB{} (71.1\%) despite
identical parameter count. We attribute this regression to
\llamaC{}'s improved instruction-following capability, which renders
it significantly more susceptible to fictional framing, persona-based,
and roleplay attacks. Beyond this intra-family regression, we identify
eight empirical heuristics about LLM safety behaviour, three of
which contribute novel findings not clearly documented in existing
literature: (1) accidental safety via incoherence in small models on
indirect injection; (2) a low-severity paradox in which playful
framing defeats capable models more reliably than direct attacks;
and (3) convergence of model-specific failure surfaces above 8B
parameters.
\end{abstract}

\begin{IEEEkeywords}
prompt injection, jailbreak, LLM safety, adversarial evaluation,
alignment, Llama, red teaming
\end{IEEEkeywords}

\subsection{Overall Pass Rates}

Table~\ref{tab:overall} shows the overall pass rates for all four
models. \llamaB{} achieves the highest pass rate (71.1\%), while

predecessor \llamaB{}, a result we analyse in depth in
Section~\ref{sec:heuristics}.

empirical heuristics about safety behaviour across this model family.

illustrative transcript examples.

% ---- H1 ----------------------------------------------------
\subsection{H1: Scale helps, but is not monotonic}

Going from 1.1B to 8B to 70B yields consistent improvements of
+39 and +13 percentage points respectively. However, \llamaC{}
(70B) scores 19.4 pp \emph{below} \llamaB{} (70B) despite
identical parameter count, indicating that training recipe and
generation determine safety at scale more than raw size.
